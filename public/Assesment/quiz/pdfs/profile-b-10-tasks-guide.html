10 Tasks to Delegate to Your First VA

A Practical Guide to Immediate ROI

Ready to start delegating but not sure where to begin? This guide identifies the top 10 tasks that deliver immediate ROI when delegated to a Virtual Assistant. Each task includes time savings, cost analysis, and step-by-step delegation instructions.

Why These 10 Tasks?

These tasks were selected based on:

  • High Time Consumption: Tasks that take 5+ hours per week
  • Low Complexity: Can be learned quickly with proper documentation
  • Immediate Impact: Free up owner/manager time for revenue-generating activities
  • Measurable ROI: Clear cost savings and efficiency gains

1Email & Calendar Management

Time Savings: 8-12 hours/week

What to Delegate:

  • Email triage and prioritization
  • Responding to routine inquiries
  • Calendar scheduling and coordination
  • Meeting reminders and follow-ups
  • Email template responses
ROI Calculation: 10 hours/week × $75/hour (owner rate) = $750/week = $3,000/month value

Delegation Steps:

  2. Set up email rules and filters
  3. Provide access to calendar with clear availability guidelines
  4. Establish daily check-in routine for urgent items

Getting Started: Your First Week

  1. Day 1-2: Choose 2-3 tasks from this list that are causing you the most pain
  2. Day 3-4: Document the process for each task (create simple checklists)
  3. Day 5: Onboard your VA and provide initial training
  4. Week 2: Start with 1 task, gradually add more as confidence builds

Best Practices for Successful Delegation

  • Start Small: Begin with 1-2 tasks, not all 10 at once
  • Document Everything: Create simple step-by-step guides
  • Set Clear Expectations: Define quality standards and deadlines
  • Regular Check-ins: Daily for first week, then weekly
  • Provide Feedback: Both positive and constructive
  • Be Patient: Allow 2-3 weeks for full proficiency
